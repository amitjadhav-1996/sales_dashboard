Amazon Superstore Sales Analysis Dashboard:

1.Project Overview:
	This project analyzes Amazon Superstore sales data to uncover key business insights related to sales performance, profitability, product categories. The dashboard is built using Power BI and demonstrates data cleaning, transformation, data modeling, DAX calculations, and interactive visualization techniques.

2.Objectives:
	1.Analyze overall sales, profit, and order performance.
	2.Identify top-performing and underperforming products.
	3.Evaluate sales and profitability by category and sub-category.
	4.Compare regional and state-wise sales performance.
	5.Monitor key business KPIs through interactive dashboards.

3.Tools & Technologies:
	1.Power BI
	2.Power Query
	3.DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
	4.Excel Dataset
	5.Data Modeling
	6.Data Preparation

4.Dashboard Features:
	1.Total Sales
	2.Total Profit
	3.Total Orders
	4.Sales Analysis
	5.Sales by Category
	6.Sales by Sub-Category
	7.Yearly Sales Trend
	8.Profit by Category
	9.Geographic Analysis
	10.Region-wise Sales
	11.State-wise Performance

5.Key Insights:
	Sales & Revenue Insights:
		1)Total Sales show a steady growth trend over time.
		2)All product categories contribute a significant portion of total revenue.
		3)Sales are often concentrated in specific Quarter due to seasonal demand.
	
	Profitability Insights:
		1)Some products generate high sales but low profit margins.
		2)Certain sub-categories contribute most of the overall profit.
		3)A small number of products are responsible for significant losses.
 	
	Product Performance Insights:
		1)A few top-selling products drive a large percentage of sales.
		2)Some products have low sales and low profit, making them candidates for discontinuation.
		3)Technology products often generate higher profits compared to some other categories.
	
	Discount Analysis:
		1)Higher discounts generally increase sales volume.
		2)Excessive discounts have negatively impacted profitability.
		3)Some products remain profitable even with discounts, while others quickly become loss-making.
		4)Strong negative correlation may exist between discount percentage and profit margin.

	Regional Analysis:
		1)East and West regions generated the highest sales revenue and Profit.
		2)Central and South regions generated the Lowest sales revenue snd Profit.
		3)Identifying underperforming regions helps target improvement strategies.

	Customer Segment Insights:
		1)Consumer customers usually contribute the highest order volume.
		2)Overall, all segments are proportional with total sales, total profit and total quantity.

	Shipping & Delivery Insights:
		1)Standard class shipping is generally the most frequently used shipping mode.
		2)Faster shipping methods may increase operational costs.
		3)High shipping costs can reduce overall profit margins.
	
	Time-Based Insights:
		1)Sales increases in Quarter 4 during year-end and holiday seasons and falls in Quarter 1 after holiday season ends.
		2)Quarterly analysis helps identify business cycles.
		3)Seasonal demand patterns assist with inventory planning.

	Category-Level Insights
		1)Technology Category contributes most to sales and profit.
		2)Office Supply category generate low revenue but higher profit compared with Furniture category.
		3)Category-level performance helps optimize inventory investment.
	
	Loss-Making Areas:
		1)Products with high discounts have generated more losses.
		2)In central region, Furniture category has generted most loss.
		3)Identifying loss-making products, categories helps improve overall business performance.

	Based on the analysis:
		1)Focus marketing efforts on high-profit categories and products.
		2)Reduce excessive discounting on low-margin products.
		3)Improve profitability in underperforming regions.
		4)Optimize inventory for top-selling products.
		5)Develop retention strategies for high-value customers.
		6)Review and potentially discontinue consistently loss-making products.
	
	6.Key Takeaways:
		1)Sales growth does not always translate into higher profits.
		2)Discounts have a significant impact on profitability.
		3)A small number of products generate a large share of revenue and profit.
		4)Data-driven decision-making can improve operational efficiency and business performance.
